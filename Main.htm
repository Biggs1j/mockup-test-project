Top Ten North American Cities to Visit

We all have our own list of locations that we would like to visit at some point in our lives. My own personal list spans across the entire globe (and is pretty extensive) but, at this particular moment, lets focus on one continent at a time.

Listed below is the Top Ten North American cities that I have an interest in visiting. Granted, a couple of these cities I have already been to but, as we all know, sometimes it takes going somewhere more than once to fully experience everything a location has to offer.

Honorable Mentions

  • San Diego , California

    • "America's Finest City"

    California's second largest city with a population of approximately 1,381, 069. Recognized as the birthplace of California.

  • Los Angeles, California

    • "City of Angels"

    The most populous city in the state of California and,the second largest city in the United States. It is known as the epicenter of the American entertainment industry.

  • Madison, Wisconsin

    • "The City of Four Lakes"

    The capital city in the state of Wisconsin, also the second largest city in the state. Consistently ranked in the media as one of the best places to live in the United States.

  • Ann Arbor, Michigan

    • "Tree Town"

    Founded in 1824, Ann Arbor is home to one of the nation's top research universities, the University of Michigan.

10.

Portland, Oregon, USA

"City of Roses"

Portland is the largest city in the state of Oregon with a population of 619,360. It is the 28th most populous city in the United States. Portland is recognized as one of the top "green" cities in the world due to its large community of bicyclists and its 10,000 acres of public parks. The town is ideal for growing roses hence, the nickname,"City of Roses". Portland also has the most total breweries and independent microbreweries in the world. Due to the plethora of brewery options and the city's frequent acclaim as one of the top beer destinations in the world, the city has also earned the nickname of "Beertown".

9.

Niagara Falls, Ontario, Canada

"The Honeymoon Capital of the World"

This Canadian city is surrounded by three large waterfalls on the Niagara River. Millions of tourists travel here every year to see these waterfalls. Niagara Falls also offers numerous amounts of entertainment options such as, casinos, theaters, souvenir shops, and restaurants.

8.

Chicago, Illinois, USA

"Second City"

With a population of over 2.7 million residents, Chicago is the third most populous city in the United States. Metro-Chicago (a.k.a. Chicagoland) is home to nearly 10 million people. For sports fans, Chicago has professional sports in each of the major professional sports leagues (basketball, baseball, football, and hockey). For shopping enthusiasts, the city offers upscale shopping along the "Magnificent Mile" and State Street.

7.

Banff, Alberta, Canada

"The Hiking Capital of Canada"

Banff is a resort town located in the Alberta Rockies in Alberta, Canada. It is known to be one of Canada's most popular tourist destinations due to its mountainous surroundings and hot springs. If one is interested in hiking, biking, and/or skiing, there are numerous hiking trails and three nearby ski resorts.

6.

New York City, New York, USA

"The City that Never Sleeps"

One of the most well known cities in the world, New York City, is the most populous city in the United States of America with a population of 8,491,079. The city is comprised of five boroughs, Brooklyn, Queens, Manhattan, the Bronx, and Staten Island. The city is considered to be the leading financial center of the world with the two largest stock exchanges in the world NYSE and NASDAQ residing here. When it comes to higher education, NYC is comprised of over 120 colleges and unversities including a few of the world's best, Columbia University, New York University, and Rockefeller University.
