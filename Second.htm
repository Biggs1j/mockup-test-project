Top Ten North American Cities to Visit

5.

San Francisco, California, USA

"The City"

San Francisco is the fourth most populous city in the U.S. state of California and, the 13th most populous city in the country (852,469).The San Francisco Bay Area is known for having the largest concentration of technology based companies within the United states. The headquarters of social media giants such as, Facebook and Twitter are stationed here. This city is also home to the world famous landmark, the Golden Gate bridge.

4.

Quebec City, Quebec, Canada

"The Gibraltar of North America"

Founded in 1608 by Samuel de Champlain, Quebec City is one of the oldest cities in North America. One of the city's most recognizable landmarks is the hotel, Chateau Frontenac, which dominates the city's skyline.

3.

Vancouver, BC, Canada

"City of Peace"

Recognized the world over as being "world-class", this seaport city (located just 2.5 hours north of Seattle) is one of the most ethically and linguistically diverse cities in Canada. With 52% of its residents having a first language other than English. Due to there being a large film production industry present within the city, Vancouver is often referred to as "Hollywood North". Consistently named in major publications as one of the top five cities worldwide for livability and quality of life. That quality of life however, comes with a hefty price as the city ranks as the most expensive city in North America.

2.

Toronto, Ontario, Canada

"Queen City"

The most populous city in the entire country of Canada (2,615,060) and the continents fourth most populous city. Toronto is a leading international centre of business, finance, arts, and culture. Much like Vancouver, Toronto is one of the world's most diverse cities with about 50% of the residents having been born outside of Canada, and over 200 distinct ethnic origins represented among its population.

1.

Seattle, Washington, USA

"The Emerald City"

Nicknamed the "The Emerald City", Seattle, WA is the largest city in the state of Washington and the entire Pacific Northwest region. This area is known for it's overcast weather, coffee, and budding technology center. It is currently the fastest growing city in the United States. Seattle has an extensive musical background. It is recognized as the birthplace of rock musician Jimi Hendrix and the alternative rock style "grunge".
